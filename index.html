More Than a Club

a century in one club

A club is born. You will run it for a hundred years.
The world will spend every one of them trying to change what it is.

What this is

You don't pick the team. You run the institution that outlives every player, manager and owner who ever passed through it.

Each era, the rules of the game change underneath you. Professionalism. Television. Foreign money. The stadium as spectacle. A choice that wins you one decade can ruin you in the next.

At the heart of your club is a charter, one line your founders swore to. The whole century is pressure on that line. Hold it and fall behind. Break it and win, and wonder what you've become.

What you balance

Three things pull against each other. Let any one hit zero and the club doesn't survive the century.

Money
The engine. It grows hungrier every era, and it always wants something in return.
Fans
The people who were here before the money, and will be here after it.
Soul
How much of the club is still the club. The charter, the place, the people. The thing money keeps trying to buy.

Win on the pitch and the trophies follow. But the dice never give you a sure thing, and a stronger squad only tilts the odds. There's always a reason to buy success now.
